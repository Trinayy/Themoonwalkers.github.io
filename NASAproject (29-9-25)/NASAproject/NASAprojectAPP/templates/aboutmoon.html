About the Moon

Gravity and Temperature

While you were there, you'd notice that the gravity on the surface of the Moon is one-sixth of Earth's, which is why astronauts appear to bounce on the Moon's surface.

The temperature on the Moon reaches about 260°F (127°C) in full Sun but drops to -280°F (-173°C) in darkness.

Water on the Moon

Initial exploration suggested the Moon was dry. In 2008, India's Chandrayaan-1 mission detected water molecules on the Moon, later confirmed by NASA's SOFIA in 2020, showing water is distributed across the surface.

Atmosphere & Magnetosphere

The Moon has a thin, weak atmosphere with no protection from radiation or meteoroids, and its magnetic field is extremely weak compared to Earth's.

Tidal Effects

The Moon's gravity causes high and low tides on Earth. Spring and neap tides occur depending on the alignment of the Sun, Moon, and Earth.

Fun Facts

  • Name: The Moon (Luna)
  • Diameter: 3,474 km (~1/4 of Earth's diameter)
  • Distance from Earth: 384,400 km
  • Orbit Period: 27.3 days
  • Gravity: 1/6th of Earth's
  • No atmosphere or weather, but ice exists in craters
  • Supermoons happen when the Moon is closest to Earth
